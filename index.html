Purpose of the event

Brain foundation models are advancing quickly, but the field is still working through basic constraints: what kinds of data are realistically available, what today's sensors can and can't capture, and what "progress" should mean in practice.

This invite-only evening brings together researchers and practitioners to compare approaches, surface disagreements, and make room for the conversations that don't happen in papers or on stage. Teams are pursuing different strategies—pushing existing sensors further, leaning on multimodal inputs, building models around specific settings. This evening puts those approaches in dialogue.

Format: Working evening with drinks and light fare. Two panels, short provocations, and substantial time for discussion.

Questions we'll explore:

  • Where is the bottleneck right now?

    Is progress primarily constrained by sensors, data access, or modeling assumptions?

  • Which workarounds seem most promising—and why?

    What strategies meaningfully move the needle under current constraints?

  • What counts as "enough" data (and of what kind)?

    When does scale help, and when are there hard limits?

  • What does "working" mean in this space?

    Which outcomes or applications feel real today, and which remain aspirational?

  • How do research and real-world systems inform one another?

    What can we learn from early deployments, and what gets lost in translation?
