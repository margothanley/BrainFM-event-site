Foundation Models
for the Brain

Laying the groundwork for the next stage in neurotech and AI

March 26, 2026 · 5–9 PM · New York City

An invite-only evening for researchers working on brain foundation models—across hardware, data, and real-world systems.

· · ·
"The most fruitful areas for the growth of the sciences were those which had been neglected as a no-man's land between the various established fields."
— Norbert Wiener, 1948

Brain foundation models are taking shape across biology, computer science, and engineering—pursued by neurotech companies, academic labs, and researchers bringing methods from fields as varied as astrophysics and deep learning. With this activity comes a set of live questions: What data do these models actually require? What hardware can produce it? Is scale the answer, or does fidelity matter more? Different groups are proposing very different solutions, and the answers are far from settled.

This convening brings researchers into the same room to compare approaches, surface disagreements, and think together about what "high-quality" should mean in practice. Two panels open the conversation; most of the evening is reserved for discussion. Attendance is by invitation—we're prioritizing researchers and practitioners working directly on these problems, though colleagues from industry and adjacent fields are welcome.
