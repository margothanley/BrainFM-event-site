March 26, 2026 · 5–9 PM · Brown Rudnick · New York City

An invite-only evening for researchers working on brain foundation models—across hardware, data, and real-world systems.

Purpose of the Event

"The most fruitful areas for the growth of the sciences were those which had been neglected as a no-man's land between the various established fields."
— Norbert Wiener, 1948

Brain foundation models are advancing quickly, but the field is still working through basic constraints: what kinds of data are realistically available, what today's sensors can and can't capture, and what "progress" should mean in practice.

This invite-only evening brings together an interdisciplinary set of researchers and practitioners to compare approaches, surface disagreements, and make room for the conversations that are hard to have in papers or on stage. The goal isn't to showcase finished results—it's to share how people are navigating the constraints of neural data and hardware, and what they think is actually working right now.

Across the field, teams are pursuing different strategies—pushing existing sensors further, leaning on multimodal inputs, drawing on non-human data, building models around specific settings, or prioritizing near-term deployment. This evening is designed to put those approaches in dialogue around a common set of questions.

Core Provocations

  • Where is the bottleneck right now? Is progress primarily constrained by sensors, data access, or modeling assumptions?
  • Which workarounds seem most promising—and why? What strategies meaningfully move the needle under current constraints?
  • What counts as "enough" data (and of what kind)? When does scale help, and when are there hard limits?
  • What does "working" mean in this space? Which outcomes or applications feel real today, and which remain aspirational?
  • How do research and real-world systems inform one another? What can we learn from early deployments, and what gets lost in translation?

A structured, conversation-forward evening with drinks and light fare—designed to maximize time for informal exchange as well as focused discussion. Attendance is by invitation.

Organizers will produce a short memo synthesizing open questions and points of convergence surfaced during the evening.
